FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

POPULAR


Do we use stretching as a warm up or cool down?
Stretching is not a warm up. In fact, stretching is best done, when the muscles are warmed up.
I am an athlete, will stretching hamper my performance?
Stretching is important to avoid muscle soreness and long term injuries. In fact, as an athlete, it is best to stretch post running, cycling etc. As, stretching decreases responsiveness to pain. While making the muscles less stable. Though, an athlete can use dynamic stretching. That is, bringing a joint repeatedly through its range of motion.
What about stretching for people who play sports like tennis, basketball, etc?
Ballistic stretches can be a favorable and an effective warm-up for sports people. (Ballistic stretching is when you take the muscle near end-range. And performing a bouncing motion. This is a type of active warm-up before a sporting event to improve muscle elasticity.) For recovery, they can practice static stretches or active stretches.
But if I am already sore; won’t stretching make me hurt more?
No! By stretching with proper form and body mechanics helps reduce soreness.
Is dynamic stretching the best type of stretching?
It depends. Dynamic stretching may be best for those who are warming up for athletic activities that require running and jumping. But in activities, where positions that demand a large range of motion are often held. Like, in case of, gymnastics, wrestling, martial arts, dance, etc. Then static stretching is preferable as it improves performance.
How often do I stretch?
Ideally, stretch every day; especially if you have a sedentary lifestyle. Stretching also depends on age, the sports you play and vulnerability to injuries or soreness.
How long do I hold the stretch?
Evidence suggests stretching anywhere from 15-60 seconds, 4-6 times per muscle group is effective.
How does long exhalations help in stretching?
Deep breathing helps in relaxing the nervous system. Which helps the connective tissues to relax. Thus, making the stretch more effective.
How does Yoga help in stretching?
It is not enough to increase the length of muscle fibers alone. A matching expansion of the connective tissue within and around the muscle is also needed. Including the overlying fascia and the wrappings of individual fibers. And this is what happens during programs of prolonged stretching. The connective tissue gradually follows the lead of the muscle fibers.Thus, the muscle as a whole gets longer and flexibility is improved. Hatha yoga stretches are a safe and effective way to bring this about.

INJURY


should I Exercise If I Have An Injury?
Injuries can bring unexpected delays to so many parts of our daily lives. We may ask ourselves, “Should I take a day off from normal activities? Do I need rest my leg to avoid further injury? Should I see my chiropractor in Anchorage for advice? Can I head to the gym after work – or should I just play a board game with the kids instead today?”
Should I Take a Pain Reliever During Workouts?
Be aware that pain medications can mask pain and allow us to feel as though we are completely better. This can hinder long-term recovery efforts. Stay mindful that muscles, joints, tendons, and ligaments may only be partway to the land of recovery.
I’m recovering from a back injury, and my doctor says that I can do light exercise. What kind of exercises does that entail?
If doctors allow you to do light exercises, it most likely means that they aren’t worried about the injury getting worse. However, you need to avoid high-impact exercises, such as burpees, jumping sets or certain variations of push-ups. You have to start light and add intensity gradually. Eventually, you’ll to return to your pre-injury routine.
How can I tell the difference between muscle and joint pain?
If you can’t tell what hurts (joint or muscle), flex the muscle. If that motion hurts the muscle and not the joint, then it’s muscle pain. After that, move your joint without flexing the muscle (you can ask someone else to help you do it). If the pain is still there then it’s joint pain.
Can stretching prevent injury?
In short, stretching doesn’t prevent injury. However, if your muscles aren’t stretched sufficiently to perform a certain set of exercises, you can get injured doing them.
